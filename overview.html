Research Overview

The semiconductor industry is booming with lots of excitement in both scientific progress and technical applications. As an undergraduate student at Cornell University, I have been working on both the theoretical and experimental aspects of semiconductor devices & physics. This ranges from understanding the material properties with powerful modern computation methods to fabricating devices in the cleanroom and characterizing them. I am mentored by Prof. Debdeep Jena at Cornell.

Project Descriptions

Two major projects I am involved in at Cornell are:

1. constructing the first tight-binding model for emerginng ultra-wide bandgap semiconductors Ga2O3 and Al2O3.

oxide tight-binding

2. developing the fabrication process and characterizing devices based on AlN-NbN semiconductor-superconductor heterostructures.

nitride superconductivity
